The Six Rules of Water Safety

What every child must know before they go near water

These rules save lives. We teach each one in every school we visit — with visuals, stories, and a pledge that students carry home.

🤝
Rule 01

Never swim alone

Always bring a buddy or swim in a supervised area. Having someone with you means help is always available if something goes wrong.

🎓
Rule 02

Be honest about your ability

Don't claim you can swim if you cannot. Overconfidence near rivers and dams is one of the leading causes of drowning among young people in Kenya.

🚫
Rule 03

Avoid rivers, dams & unmonitored pools

These environments are unpredictable. Currents change, depths are hidden, and there is no lifeguard present. Choose monitored pools only.

👁️
Rule 04

Always swim under supervision

A qualified adult or lifeguard must be present every time you swim — not just nearby, but actively watching. This is non-negotiable.

🆘
Rule 05

Know how to call for help

Shout clearly, wave your arm, and never dive in to save someone unless you are trained. Throwing a rope or object is often safer.

🌊
Rule 06

Respect the water

Water does not care how brave or strong you are. Treat it with respect, follow the rules, and swimming becomes one of life's greatest joys.

What we do in schools

A 45-minute session that changes how children think about water

Our certified instructors and lifeguards deliver every session — free of charge, in your hall or classroom.

Minutes 0 – 5

Introduction & icebreaker

A fun quiz — "Who thinks they can swim?" — opens the conversation about confidence vs real skill.

Minutes 5 – 15

The dangers of water

Real Kenyan examples: rivers, dams, and unmonitored pools. We make the risk real without making it scary.

Minutes 15 – 25

The six safety rules

Visual posters and group discussion. Children say each rule aloud and explain it in their own words.

Minutes 25 – 35

Live demonstration

Our instructors use gestures and visuals to show floating technique, rescue calls, and safe behaviour near water.

Minutes 35 – 45

Pupil pledge & Q&A

Each child takes the Water Safety Promise and receives a pledge card to bring home to their family.

🌊

The Water Safety Promise

I promise to always swim with a buddy, to be honest about my ability, to avoid rivers and dams, and to respect the power of water. I will look out for my friends — and ask for help when I need it.
